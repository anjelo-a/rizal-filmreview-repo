# Introduction

"Rizal in Dapitan" (1997) dramatizes a crucial period in the life of Jose Rizal — his exile in Dapitan (1892–1896). The film explores Rizal not only as the national figure often presented in history books, but as a complex human being: a teacher, a physician, an engineer, and a man who formed meaningful relationships in a provincial community.

This review aims to summarize the narrative, highlight notable cinematic and historical aspects, and offer a critical reflection on the film's treatment of Rizal's life and legacy.
